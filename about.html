The Monkees band grew directly out of The Monkees TV series which aired for 2 seasons. The American TV sitcom portrayed an up and coming band (The Monkees) as they tried to make their way into mainstream Rock and Roll. After two Emmy Awards in 1967, the producers called the show quits in spring 1968. After the show ended, the four actors took their music from the TV set to the stage. Already with number of top ten hits, the band continued to produce and perform nationally and globally for thousands of fans. By the time the band dissolved in 1971, they had produced three number one hits, six top ten singles, and sold over 35 million albums in 1967 alone. Some of The Monkees greatest hits include I'm a believer, Last Train to Clarksville,and Daydream Believer.
